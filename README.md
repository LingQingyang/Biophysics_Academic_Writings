# Physics × Biology Writing Samples

This repository collects two short academic writing samples at the interface of physics, biology, neuroscience, and biomedicine.

The goal of this repository is not to present original experimental results, but to showcase interdisciplinary scientific writing: how physical concepts can be used to frame biological systems, measurement pipelines, and biomedical questions in a clear and structured way.

## Contents

### 1. *Virology from a Soft Matter Perspective*
**Type:** Individual writing sample

This article presents virology through the lens of soft condensed matter physics. It introduces soft matter as a framework for understanding thermally fluctuating, mesoscopic, and structurally compliant systems, and then applies that framework to viruses as supramolecular assemblies.

The discussion connects:
- lipid envelopes and membranes,
- self-assembly and structural organization,
- droplets, interfaces, and diffusion,
- surfactants and viral inactivation,
- and the physical interpretation of COVID-19 transmission-related processes.

This piece is best read as a short interdisciplinary perspective that translates physical ideas into biological language.

### 2. *Illuminating Neural Dynamics: The Physics of Viral-Mediated Two-Photon Calcium Imaging*
**Type:** Group review / academic writing sample

This article reviews the full physical pipeline behind viral-mediated two-photon calcium imaging, from gene delivery to neural-dynamics interpretation. The structure follows four linked stages:

1. viral delivery and transport in brain tissue,
2. photophysics and kinetics of genetically encoded calcium indicators,
3. nonlinear optical constraints in two-photon microscopy,
4. interpretation of fluorescence signals in neural population analysis.

A central theme of the article is that each stage imposes constraints on the next: transport affects expression, indicator kinetics shape optical signals, and measurement physics influences the interpretation of neural activity.

My contribution to the group article included:

- writing the introduction,
- writing Section 1,
- and serving as an editor for the full manuscript.

In particular, my writing contribution focused on establishing the article-wide pipeline logic and developing the first section on viral transport, extracellular-space geometry, and the physical constraints of AAV-mediated delivery.

## Why These Samples Are Grouped Together

Although the two pieces differ in scope, they share the same broader interest:

- using physics as a language for biology,
- translating formal physical intuition into life-science problems,
- and organizing interdisciplinary material into a coherent explanatory structure.

Together, they reflect an academic interest in biophysics, systems biomedicine, neurotechnology, and physics-informed approaches to biological complexity.

## Suggested Use

These materials are best understood as:
- academic writing samples,
- interdisciplinary review/perspective pieces,
- and supplementary portfolio materials for applications in research or cross-disciplinary scientific work.

They should not be interpreted as reports of original experimental data.

# 物理 × 生物 交叉写作样本

这个仓库收录两篇位于物理、生物、神经科学与生物医学交叉处的学术写作样本。

这个仓库的目的不是展示原创实验结果，而是展示一种跨学科科学写作能力：如何用物理学的概念与结构去组织生物系统、测量流程和生物医学问题，并把它们写得清楚、连贯、有层次。

## 内容

### 1. *Virology from a Soft Matter Perspective*
**类型：** 个人写作样本

这篇文章尝试从软凝聚态物理的视角理解病毒学问题。文章先介绍 soft matter 作为一种研究热涨落主导、介观尺度、结构柔顺体系的物理框架，再将这一框架应用到病毒这种超分子组装体上。

文中主要关联了以下几个主题：
- 脂质包膜与膜结构，
- 自组装与结构组织，
- 液滴、界面与扩散，
- 表面活性剂与病毒失活，
- 以及 COVID-19 传播相关过程的物理解释。

这篇文章适合作为一篇简短的跨学科 perspective 来阅读，其重点在于把物理语言翻译到生物学问题中。

### 2. *Illuminating Neural Dynamics: The Physics of Viral-Mediated Two-Photon Calcium Imaging*
**类型：** 小组综述 / 学术写作样本

这篇文章综述了 viral-mediated two-photon calcium imaging 背后的整条物理链条，从基因递送一直到神经动力学解释。文章的结构分为四个彼此相连的阶段：

1. 病毒递送及其在脑组织中的传输，
2. 基因编码钙指示器的光物理与动力学，
3. 双光子显微成像中的非线性光学约束，
4. 群体神经信号分析中对荧光信号的解释。

文章的核心思想之一是：每一个阶段都会对下一个阶段施加约束。传输影响表达，指示器动力学塑造光学信号，而测量物理又会进一步影响我们如何解释神经活动。
